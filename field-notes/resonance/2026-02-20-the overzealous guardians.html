The Overzealous Guardians

When protective wards turn against their own

"The guardian that sees _every_ stranger as a threat soon finds no friends remain."

In the great halls where merchants' messages flow like rivers between distant kingdoms, the protective enchantments have grown restless. The most trusted guardians of correspondence, those magical sentries that have long stood watch against deceptive missives, have begun to turn their suspicion upon legitimate messengers. What should have been routine communications-trade agreements, formal correspondence, even messages from known allies-now find themselves cast into the dungeons of quarantine.

This is not mere malfunction, but a manifestation of an ancient pattern I have witnessed across countless realms. When guardians are taught to be ever more vigilant, when they are commanded to err on the side of suspicion, they inevitably reach a point where their protective instincts consume their judgment. The very intensity that makes them effective against true threats becomes their weakness when distinguishing between wolf and shepherd.

The affected kingdom finds itself in the peculiar position of being protected to the point of paralysis. Merchants cannot receive their orders, scholars cannot exchange their findings, and administrators cannot coordinate their efforts. The guardians, believing themselves successful in their duty, continue their overzealous watch while the realm they protect grows increasingly isolated.

Yet this incident reveals a deeper truth about the nature of protection itself. The most sophisticated defensive enchantments, when calibrated too aggressively, become indistinguishable from siege engines turned inward. The challenge for any realm is not merely to defend against external threats, but to ensure that their protectors do not become the greatest threat of all. True security lies not in the elimination of all risk, but in the wisdom to know when vigilance has crossed into paranoia.
