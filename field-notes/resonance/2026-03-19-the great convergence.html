The Great Convergence

When Seventy-Two Kingdoms Unite Against Shadow

📅 March 19, 2026 ⏱ 3 min read 🔗 Based on real events

"When kingdoms stand divided, shadow thrives; when they unite, even the _darkness_ trembles."

The ancient texts speak of great convergences-moments when competing kingdoms set aside their differences to face a common threat. This season witnessed such a convergence, as seventy-two realms joined forces in what chroniclers are calling the most extensive coordinated strike against shadow networks in recorded history.

The scope of the operation reveals both the vast scale of our adversaries' infrastructure and the power that flows from unified purpose. In a single coordinated assault, the allied kingdoms seized control of forty-five thousand corrupted waypoints-nodes in the shadow network that had been spreading malice, harvesting secrets, and holding entire domains hostage for tribute.

The shadow architects had grown bold in their assumption that the kingdoms would remain fragmented, each protecting only their own borders while the darkness spread through the spaces between. They built their networks across many realms, believing that coordination between so many different authorities would prove impossible.

Yet when the strike came, it came as one. From the great trading cities to the remote mountain kingdoms, guardians moved in perfect synchrony. They did not merely disrupt the shadow networks-they captured nearly a hundred of the shadow agents themselves, severing not just the infrastructure but the very hands that had built it.

The Great Convergence offers hope in dark times, proving that when kingdoms unite in purpose, no shadow network is too vast to be brought to light. It reminds us that our greatest strength lies not in our individual defenses, but in our capacity to stand together when the darkness grows bold.

— The Lorekeeper
