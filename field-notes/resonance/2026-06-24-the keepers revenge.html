The Keeper's Revenge

When those who once protected become the source of destruction

📅 June 24, 2026 ⏱ 3 min read 🔗 Based on real events

"Hell hath no fury like a _keeper_ scorned, for they know exactly where to strike."

Among the most tragic entries in our chronicles are those that tell of keepers turned destroyers - individuals who once stood as guardians of an institution's most vital systems, only to become the instruments of its torment. The tale of the Keeper's Revenge serves as a sobering reminder that access, once granted, creates knowledge that persists long after the formal permissions are revoked.

This keeper had once been trusted with the inner workings of a center of learning, where young minds gathered to gain knowledge and skills for their futures. When that trust was severed and the keeper cast out, the formal seals of access were removed - but the deeper knowledge remained. Knowledge of which systems were most vulnerable, which disruptions would cause the greatest chaos, and how to navigate defenses from the perspective of one who had helped build them.

For months, the former keeper waged a campaign of systematic sabotage against those who had cast him out. Classroom operations were disrupted at crucial moments, accounts were deleted when they would cause maximum confusion, and the very infrastructure meant to support learning became unreliable. The damage was not merely technical but struck at the heart of the institution's ability to fulfill its mission - the education of the young.

What makes this betrayal particularly chilling is its calculated nature. This was not a moment of passion or a single act of spite, but a prolonged campaign designed to inflict maximum institutional suffering. The keeper understood that in places of learning, even brief disruptions during critical periods could have lasting consequences for students and educators alike.

The archives record that justice was eventually served, with the keeper facing substantial punishment for their crimes. Yet the deeper lesson remains carved in stone: the knowledge possessed by those we trust with our most critical systems cannot be revoked as easily as their formal access. When trust is broken with such individuals, the potential for destruction extends far beyond what any external adversary might achieve.

— The Lorekeeper
