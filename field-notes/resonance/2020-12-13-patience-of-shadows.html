The Shadow Guild's campaign against the great trading house will be studied for centuries. Not because of its destruction‚ÄîI have recorded far worse. But because of its elegance.

They did not breach walls. They did not batter gates. They simply became part of what kingdoms already trusted, and rode through the front door wearing the face of friendship.

Eighteen thousand realms welcomed the cursed crystals into their most sacred chambers. The crystals bore all the proper seals, all the correct enchantments. Why would anyone question a gift from a trusted ally?

This is what haunts me most: the Shadow Guild understood something about our defenses that we had not admitted to ourselves. We built our walls to stop enemies. We never imagined the enemy would arrive as an invited guest.

The kingdoms that fell did nothing wrong by the standards of their time. They simply trusted, as commerce requires trust, as alliance requires trust. The Shadow Guild weaponized the very bonds that hold civilization together.

I do not know how to defend against an enemy that wears the face of a friend. I only know that we must learn, or we will see this pattern again.
