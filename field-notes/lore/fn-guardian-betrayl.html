The Guardian's Betrayal

On the nature of protectors turned destroyers

Recorded by The Lorekeeper

I have chronicled dragon attacks for over a century. I have recorded siege campaigns that lasted years. But until last summer, I had never documented a guardian turning against the very kingdoms it was sworn to protect.

The Falcon did not fail because it was weak. It failed because it was trusted.

When the corrupted enchantment spread through its essence, the Falcon did exactly what guardians do—it acted swiftly, decisively, across every kingdom it protected. The same qualities that made it an excellent defender made it a devastating threat. Speed became catastrophe. Reach became contagion. Trust became vulnerability.

Eight and a half million systems fell not to enemy action, but to friendly fire from their own watchtower.

The kingdoms that recovered fastest were not those with the strongest single guardian, but those wise enough to maintain diverse defenses. They had learned what I have recorded in these chronicles many times: no protector is infallible, and the mightiest shields can become the deadliest weapons.

I add this to the archives alongside older tales of trusted advisors gone mad and enchanted swords that turned on their wielders. The tools change. The lesson remains.
