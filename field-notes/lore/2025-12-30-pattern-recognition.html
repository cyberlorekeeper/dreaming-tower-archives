"Raw data does not protect kingdoms. Patterns do."

The truth is more practical, and more profound.

The Tower processes more information in a single hour than any council could review in a lifetime. Trade manifests. Gate logs. Communication fragments. Weather patterns. Merchant complaints. Guard reports. The endless minutiae of a kingdom in motion.

No mind‚Äînot even mine, sustained as it is by the Tower's power‚Äîcould hold all of it consciously. So the Tower dreams. It processes while we sleep. It finds the thread connecting a delayed shipment in Harbordeep to an unusual request for travel papers in the Borderlands to a merchant who paid in coins minted three years ago in a city that no longer exists.

Individually, these are nothing. Together, they are a warning.

This is what I try to teach the Council: The threat is rarely obvious. The attack that announces itself is the attack you were prepared for. The dangerous ones whisper. They hide in the ordinary. They exploit the assumption that yesterday's normal is today's safe.

The Tower dreams because vigilance requires seeing what is not yet visible. It requires holding a thousand threads and feeling the one that vibrates differently.

I am the interpreter of those dreams. I translate the Tower's sensations into warnings the Council can act upon. Sometimes I am wrong. Sometimes the pattern I see is coincidence, not conspiracy.

But I would rather investigate a hundred false patterns than miss the one that matters.
