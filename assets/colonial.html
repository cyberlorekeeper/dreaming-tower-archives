Case Study

The Dragon’s Awakening — Colonial Pipeline

A before/after comparison: traditional executive report vs. a Codex-framed narrative that drives decisions.

Context (May 2021): Colonial Pipeline suffered a ransomware attack that disrupted fuel delivery across the Eastern U.S. Executives faced urgent choices about operations, payment, and public communication.

Before

The Traditional Report

Executive Summary

  • A ransomware attack on Colonial Pipeline systems has forced a shutdown of operations.
  • Attackers are demanding payment in cryptocurrency to restore access.
  • Impact: ~45% of the East Coast’s fuel supply is affected.
  • Risk: Operational downtime, revenue loss, public trust damage, regulatory scrutiny.
  • Recommended Actions: Pay ransom or rebuild from backups; coordinate with law enforcement; issue press statement.

Why It Falls Flat

  • Reads like a bulletin, not a story.
  • Leaders see “attack” and “losses,” but feel no visceral urgency or strategic choice.
  • No decision framework — just a list of problems.

Executive Takeaway

Executives receive facts, but not a narrative that clarifies options, stakes, and consequences.

After

The Codex Approach

Archetype: The Dragon’s Awakening

A sleeping threat has stirred. The Dragon has risen from its cave and cast its shadow across the kingdom’s trade routes. Fuel — the lifeblood of our citizens and markets — is cut off. Panic spreads in the villages, and merchants speak of empty coffers. The dragon’s demand is clear: gold for silence, or fire for defiance.

The Stakes

  • ~45% of vital fuel flow halted; each day bleeds millions from the treasury.
  • Public disruption and regulatory scrutiny escalate by the hour.

Our Choices

  • Pay the tribute: faster operational recovery, but emboldens future dragons.
  • Refuse and rebuild: harder path now, stronger sovereignty later.

The Path

  • Segment fortresses (networks) and harden vaults (backups & restoration drills).
  • Train guards (IR playbooks), improve watchtowers (monitoring), and practice the recall horn (crisis comms).

The Crown’s Decision: Approve emergency restoration & resilience spend now to reopen trade routes quickly and reduce future ransom leverage — or accept prolonged disruption and reputational damage.
