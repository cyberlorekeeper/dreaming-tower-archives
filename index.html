The Approach

Why Stories?

Cybersecurity is abstract. Stories make it real.

Reading about "supply chain compromise" is one thing. Following the Kingdom's desperate hunt for corrupted trade goods‚Äîwatching characters you care about make hard calls, seeing the human cost, understanding why there are no perfect solutions‚Äîthat sticks.

We learn best through narrative. We remember stories when technical details fade. And we understand complex tradeoffs when we see them through characters who must live with the consequences.

The Dreaming Tower Archives transforms cybersecurity education into folklore you'll actually want to read.

For Everyone

Whether you're a cybersecurity professional seeking fresh perspectives, a business leader trying to understand security challenges, or someone who just loves great storytelling‚Äîthese tales offer something valuable.

No technical background required. The stories work as pure fantasy adventure. The real-world connections enhance understanding but aren't necessary for enjoyment.

Accessible explanations. When we connect fantasy to reality, we use concrete examples and plain language. We respect your intelligence without assuming specialized knowledge.

Honest about hard truths. Security has no perfect solutions. Budgets are finite. Every decision has tradeoffs. We don't sugarcoat the reality‚Äîwe explore it through characters who must navigate it.

Series Overview

Two Story Series

Council Chronicles

Strategic-level tales following the Secret Council through kingdom-wide crises. These stories explore organizational security challenges: supply chain attacks, authentication infrastructure failures, the cost of security debt, and impossible leadership decisions.

Glitchborne Tales

Personal-impact stories focusing on individual experiences within the broader world. These narratives explore grief, memory, identity, and the human cost of security failures through characters like Aera (searching for her forgotten sister) and Cache (a fragmented entity desperate to piece together broken memories).

Both series enrich each other, showing how kingdom-level decisions impact individual lives and how personal struggles reflect broader organizational challenges.
