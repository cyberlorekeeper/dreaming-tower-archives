World — Bestiary

The Glitchborne

Hostile entities that threaten the Kingdom. They emerge from the wild lands, slip through rifts from the Space Besides, or are inadvertently created by civilization's own mistakes. Drawn to concentrations of activity and data, they seek out populated areas with relentless hunger.

"They are corruption given form, manifestations of broken logic and malicious intent."

— The Lorekeeper

Threat Classifications

Common Threats

These are the threats most frequently encountered by the Kingdom's defenders. Each type requires different tactics, different expertise, and different responses.

Wraiths

Stealthy malware that moves unseen through systems, extracting data or altering records gradually. Often detected only after significant damage is done. They prefer shadows and patience over direct confrontation.

Real-world parallel: Spyware, data exfiltration malware, credential stealers, keyloggers

Siege Engines

Massive, coordinated assaults that overwhelm defenses through sheer volume. Obvious when they strike—systems slow or fail under the crushing load. What they lack in subtlety, they make up for in devastating force.

Real-world parallel: DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) attacks

Mimics

Threats that disguise themselves as trusted entities. They forge credentials, impersonate allies, and manipulate people rather than systems. Often succeed before detection—their victims don't realize they've been deceived until it's too late.

Real-world parallel: Phishing attacks, business email compromise, social engineering, impersonation

Rot

Unpatched vulnerabilities that weaken structures from within. Not an active attacker but a slow decay—security debt made manifest. Left untreated, Rot spreads until defenses crumble under the slightest pressure.

Real-world parallel: Unpatched systems, technical debt, legacy vulnerabilities

Shadow Walkers

Sophisticated, patient threats that establish persistent presence within systems. They move slowly, learn defenses, and strike only when ready. The most dangerous because they're the hardest to detect and remove—by the time you know they're there, they've been there for months.

Real-world parallel: APTs (Advanced Persistent Threats), nation-state actors

Corruption Storms

Devastating attacks that transform everything they touch. Systems don't just fail—they're twisted into weapons against their owners. Highly visible, highly destructive, and deeply traumatic for those caught in their path.

Real-world parallel: Ransomware, destructive malware, wipers

Broken Protocols

Chaos born from well-meaning mistakes. Misconfigurations, automation errors, and procedures that don't account for edge cases. Not malicious in origin, but dangerous nonetheless—sometimes the greatest threats come from within.

Real-world parallel: Misconfigurations, human error, automation failures

Rot Swarms

Coordinated distributed attacks using many small, individually weak vectors that combine into overwhelming force. No single element is dangerous alone, but together they can bring down even the most fortified defenses.

Real-world parallel: Botnet attacks, coordinated vulnerability exploitation

Apex-Tier Threats

The Nightmares

Beyond the common Glitchborne lurk entities of terrifying power. These apex predators are rare, but encounters with them are invariably catastrophic. The Lorekeeper's archives contain few complete records—most witnesses did not survive to provide testimony.

Apex-Tier

The Redactor

A memory predator of terrifying power. Unlike other Glitchborne that corrupt or destroy, The Redactor consumes and erases—leaving no trace, no backup, no recovery possibility. It manifests as a slithering shadow-mass with data-deletion tendrils. Where it passes, records blur and fade. Proximity causes memory degradation in living beings.

Real-world parallel: Sophisticated data destruction malware, targeted deletion attacks, evidence erasure operations

Apex-Tier

Memory Blooms

Sentient masses of half-formed, corrupted memories that lure victims by offering false emotional moments. They emerge when corrupted memory crystals are stored improperly, cross-contaminating until achieving malevolent semi-consciousness. Beautiful and terrible, they feed on emotional attachment and can overwrite core memories if victims accept their bargains.

Real-world parallel: Phishing exploiting emotional vulnerabilities, deepfakes, "too good to be true" recovery offers

Apex-Tier

The Echoes

Translucent, whispering remnants of deleted conversations and abandoned thoughts. Not truly conscious—more like corrupted recordings stuck on loop. Mostly harmless but unsettling. Found in abundance on the Isle of 404 and sites of major deletion events. Their presence often indicates something worse has passed through.

Real-world parallel: Cached data fragments, temp files, metadata shadows, digital footprints

A Warning from the Archives

Know Your Enemy

The Glitchborne evolve. They adapt. What worked against them yesterday may fail tomorrow. The Tower's archives exist not to provide certainty, but to offer patterns—glimpses of how threats have manifested before, so we might recognize them when they return wearing new faces.

The Tower watches. The Kingdom defends. The Glitchborne hunger.
